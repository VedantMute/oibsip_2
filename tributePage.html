Introduction

M.S. Dhoni, in full Mahendra Singh Dhoni, (born July 7, 1981, Ranchi, Bihar [now Jharkhand] state, India), Indian cricketer whose rise to prominence in the early 21st century culminated in his captaincy of the Indian national team that won the one-day Cricket World Cup in 2011. Dhoni made his international debut in 2004. MS Dhoni is a former Indian cricketer and captain of the Indian national team in limited-overs formats from 2007 to 2017, and in Test cricket from 2008 to 20141. He is also the current captain of Chennai Super Kings in the Indian Premier League1. He is widely regarded as one of the best finishers and wicket-keepers in world cricket2. Under his captaincy, India won three major ICC trophies: the 2007 World Twenty20, the 2011 Cricket World Cup, and the 2013 Champions Trophy.

Timeline

  • 2004 Dhoni makes his international debut against Bangladesh in an ODI at Chittagong.
  • 2005 Promoted up the order to accelerate scoring, Dhoni smashes an unbeaten 183 off 145 balls, the then record of the highest individual score in ODI cricket in the second innings.
  • 2005 Dhoni makes his Test debut against Sri Lanka in Chennai.
  • 2008 Dhoni leads India to its first-ever bilateral ODI series victory in Sri Lanka.
  • 2008 Dhoni makes his debut as full-time Test captain of India during the fourth and final Test against Australia at Nagpur, replaces Anil Kumble who was injured in the third Test.
  • 2009 Under Dhoni's captaincy, India tops the ICC Test cricket rankings for the first time.
  • 2013 Under Dhoni's captaincy, India beats Australia 4-0 at home for the first time in a Test series.
  • 2018 Under Dhoni's captaincy, Chennai Super Kings win their third IPL title by defeating Sunrisers Hyderabad by eight wickets.

Awards

  • 2018 Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award
  • 2009 Major Dhyanchand Khel Ratna award, India's highest honor given for achievement in sports
  • 2007–08 Major Dhyanchand Khel Ratna award, India's highest honor given for achievement in sports
  • 2011–2020 ICC Men's ODI team of the decade
